How Your Plans Are Made

RootPlans uses a combination of structured horticultural data and AI-generated text to produce garden plans. This page explains what's behind each recommendation.

AI Generation

Language Model

Garden plan text — including plant descriptions, care instructions, companion planting notes, and layout suggestions — is generated by Claude, a large language model developed by Anthropic. Claude is trained on a broad range of horticultural, botanical, and agricultural literature.

Data Sources

Horticultural Knowledge Base

Recommendations are grounded in:

  • USDA Plant Hardiness Zone Map data for zone-appropriate plant selection
  • Cooperative Extension Service guidelines from land-grant universities
  • Companion planting research and integrated pest management (IPM) principles
  • Native plant databases for regionally appropriate species
  • Traditional and ethnobotanical records for functional and medicinal plant notes
What AI Does Not Do

Limitations

AI-generated plans should be treated as informed starting points, not professional advice. The following are outside the scope of automated generation:

  • Soil testing or site-specific soil chemistry analysis
  • Local microclimate assessment (frost pockets, wind corridors, drainage)
  • Precise spacing for your specific bed dimensions
  • Medical or clinical claims — any wellness notes use traditional-use language only
Verification

Our Recommendation

Cross-reference plant selections with your local Cooperative Extension office or Master Gardener program before committing to a large planting. Conditions vary significantly even within a single USDA zone.
